The Foundations of Healthy Eating

Macronutrients: The Building Blocks of Nutrition

Macronutrients are nutrients your body needs in large quantities for energy and proper functioning.

  • Proteins: Essential for building and repairing tissues, muscles, and organs
  • Carbohydrates: The body's main source of energy, especially for the brain
  • Fats: Important for energy, hormone production, and absorption of certain vitamins

Micronutrients: Essential Vitamins and Minerals

Although needed in smaller quantities, micronutrients are crucial for many bodily functions:

  • Support the immune system
  • Enable energy production from food
  • Maintain bone health and strength
  • Support brain functions and nervous system
  • Act as antioxidants to protect cells from damage

The Vital Role of Hydration

Water is essential for almost all bodily functions:

  • Regulates body temperature
  • Helps transport nutrients throughout the body
  • Facilitates digestion and prevents constipation
  • Lubricates joints and protects organs
  • Helps eliminate waste

Aim to drink at least 8 glasses (2 liters) of water per day, more if you're active.

Create Your Perfect Meal Plan

Balanced Plate Guidelines

  • ½ plate: Vegetables and fruits
  • ¼ plate: Whole grains
  • ¼ plate: Lean proteins
  • Include healthy fats in moderation

Sample Meal Plans

Weight management: Greek yogurt, grilled chicken salad, baked salmon

Energy boost: Oatmeal, hummus wrap, tofu stir-fry

Muscle building: Eggs and toast, turkey breast, lean steak

Quick Tips

  • Fill half your plate with vegetables
  • Choose whole grains over refined ones
  • Include lean protein sources
  • Limit added sugars and salt
  • Eat slowly and savor each bite
  • Plan your meals in advance
